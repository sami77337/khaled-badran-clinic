# Data Exposure Matrix

Batch 10 privacy and data-surface inventory for Dr. Khaled Badran Clinic after
Batch 9.

This document describes current implemented data exposure. It is not legal
advice and does not replace privacy/legal review. No real patient data should be
seeded, committed, copied into docs, or used in screenshots/logs.

## Privacy Summary

- Public booking remains login-free.
- Public booking success uses UUID `public_token` routes.
- Patient portal appointment detail requires login and ownership filtering.
- Patient portal pages are no-cache.
- Staff operational data stays behind staff-only authorization.
- Medical records, uploads, WhatsApp message storage, and payment features are
  not implemented.
- Future WhatsApp messaging must not carry detailed medical information before a
  separate approved design exists.

Current pages:

- `/book/`
- `/book/visit-type/`
- `/book/slots/`
- `/book/confirm/`
- English equivalents under `/en/book/`.

Patient-safe fields visible on public booking pages:

| Field or data | Source | Exposure status | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Clinic public name | `ClinicProfile` or fallback constants | Public-safe | Public clinic identity only. |
| Public doctor display name | `Doctor.display_name_ar/en` | Public-safe | Public profile field. |
| Public doctor specialty | `Doctor.specialty_ar/en` | Public-safe | Public profile field. |
| Public visit type name | `VisitType.name_ar/en` | Public-safe | Active visit types only. |
| Visit duration | `VisitType.duration_minutes` | Public-safe | Used for slot display and scheduling. |
| Patient-visible price | `VisitType.patient_visible_price` | Public-safe only if configured visible | Seed data currently leaves prices null and hidden. |
| Visit instructions | `VisitType.instructions_ar/en` | Public-safe | Public instructions only. |
| Available date/time slots | Generated by `apps.booking.services` | Public-safe operational metadata | Derived from active schedules, closed days, settings, and existing appointment blocking. |
| Booking form full name input | Patient-submitted POST field | Public booking input | Rendered in the form; creates a patient only on valid final confirmation. |
| Booking form phone input | Patient-submitted POST field | Public booking input | Normalized server-side; raw phone must not appear in cache keys. |
| Booking form WhatsApp phone input | Patient-submitted POST field | Public booking input | Stored only with appointment patient contact data; no WhatsApp sending exists. |
| Optional booking note input | Patient-submitted POST field | Public booking input | Staff/internal after submission; must not appear on public success or portal patient pages. |

Public booking pages must not expose:

- existing patient appointment lists,
- internal appointment IDs,
- audit logs,
- status history,
- staff operation URLs,
- staff notes,
- medical records,
- uploads/private media,
- WhatsApp messages,
- payment details.

Patient-safe fields visible in the patient portal:

| Masked username/phone | `services.masked_account_identifier(user.username)` | Patient-limited | Raw phone username is not shown on account page. |

Patient portal access controls:

- Dashboard/list/detail/account/password/link pages require login.
- Appointment detail filters by `public_token` and `patient__user=request.user`.
- User A receives 404 for User B's appointment UUID.
- Numeric portal appointment detail URLs are not routed.
- Portal pages are no-cache.

## Account Recovery Limitation

Current account recovery is intentionally limited:

- `GET /portal/account-recovery/` and `/en/portal/account-recovery/` are static
  informational policy pages.
- The route does not collect phone, email, symptoms, records, images, reports,
  or other sensitive information.
- The route does not send email.
- The route does not send WhatsApp messages.
- The route does not create support tickets.
- The route does not disclose whether a phone number or email has an account.
- `POST` is rejected by `require_GET`.

Production launch still needs a reviewed recovery process before any
self-service password reset or contact-update workflow is enabled.

## Data Deletion Limitation

The current implementation does not provide a self-service deletion workflow.
Documentation recognizes deletion requests as future/privacy work, but no
automated deletion feature is implemented.

Before launch, the project needs:

- legal/privacy review of retention obligations,
- an approved patient identity verification policy,
- a deletion/account closure intake and review process,
- audit retention and access review policy,
- documented boundaries for whether appointment, audit, and future medical
  records may be deleted, retained, anonymized, or restricted.

Do not implement automated deletion of clinical or operational records without
legal/privacy review and explicit approval.

## Legal and Privacy Review Requirement

Before public launch or any broader patient portal launch:

- privacy policy must be reviewed,
- terms must be reviewed,
- medical disclaimer must be reviewed,
- WhatsApp policy must be reviewed before WhatsApp integration,
- retention/deletion workflow must be reviewed,
- patient identity verification must be defined,
- account recovery must be defined,
- monitoring and abuse response must be defined,
- local legal/privacy obligations must be assessed.

The current legal pages are operational drafts, not final legal approval.

## Seed and Commit Data Policy

No real patient data should be seeded or committed.

Allowed seed data:

- public clinic placeholder content,
- public doctor placeholder/profile content requiring final verification,
- visit types,
- booking settings,
- demo schedules.

Not allowed in seed data or Git:

- real patient names,
- real patient phone numbers,
- real emails,
- real appointment histories,
- medical notes,
- diagnoses,
- reports,
- images,
- videos,
- audio,
- private media,
- WhatsApp messages,
- payment records,
- credentials,
- `.env` values,
- logs with sensitive content.

## WhatsApp Medical Information Boundary

WhatsApp sending and webhooks are not implemented.

WhatsApp must not carry detailed medical information before a separate approved
design exists.

Before any WhatsApp integration is designed or built:

- do not send detailed medical information over WhatsApp,
- do not send diagnoses, triage decisions, treatment plans, reports, images, or
  sensitive records through automated WhatsApp flows,
- define consent and opt-out rules,
- define message logging boundaries,
- define provider credential handling,
- define cost/abuse controls,
- define webhook verification,
- define incident response and token rotation,
- complete privacy/legal review.

Until that design is approved, WhatsApp must remain an informational policy page
and placeholder contact concept only.
